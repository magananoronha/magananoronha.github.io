why even get a PhD

As a bright-eyed geology undergrad I read a paper one day and stumbled on the complexities of radiocarbon dating and became obsessed with the huge gaps in our knowledge of the dating system. Nothing seemed more important or impactful to me than improving the tool that was key to our understanding of recent Earth and human history, so I went and got a whole PhD on the topic. I loved what I did and never ever have I regretted the time I spent in academia. I made real contributions to a topic that I was passionate about and was given opportunities to do things and grow in ways that are very hard to come by outside of an academic environment.

I didn't go to graduate school to become a professor - I never ever wanted to pursue the tenure track. It's always been important to me to live where I want to live and to primarily spend my days doing science rather than teaching or doing administrative work. I always planned to go into a researcher position, and when the term ran out on my postdoc I had two strong leads for a couple of those, including one that was funded with hard money doing a very exciting data science work, but I ultimately opted to pursue a position working in data science in the software space.
